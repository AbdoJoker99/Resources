Theoretical Limitations of Accuracy

In reality, it's impossible to achieve 100% accuracy with a machine learning model. The maximum possible accuracy theoretically for any system is known as the Bayes Optimal Error (also known as Bayes Error Rate).

As shown in the graph below, the accuracy increases rapidly until it reaches the Human-level performance threshold. After that, the rate of accuracy improvement slows down.

Accuracy vs Time graph

(Image source: https://rb.gy/8kivby)

Practical Considerations

While it's theoretically impossible to reach 100% accuracy, the actual achievable accuracy depends on various factors, such as:

Dataset quality and complexity: The accuracy of your model is limited by the quality and complexity of your dataset.
Model architecture and hyperparameters: The choice of model architecture and hyperparameters can significantly impact the achievable accuracy.
Evaluation metrics: The choice of evaluation metric(s) can influence the perceived accuracy of your model.

Realistic Expectations

In practice, it's essential to set realistic expectations for your model's accuracy. Aiming for an accuracy of 95% or higher may not always be achievable or necessary, depending on the problem you're trying to solve.
